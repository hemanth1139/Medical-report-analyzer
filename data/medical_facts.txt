Normal fasting blood glucose range is 70 to 99 mg/dL. Above 126 mg/dL on two tests indicates diabetes.
HbA1c below 5.7 percent is normal. Between 5.7 and 6.4 is prediabetes. Above 6.5 is diabetes.
Normal LDL cholesterol is below 100 mg/dL. Above 160 is high risk for heart disease.
Normal HDL for men is above 40 mg/dL. For women above 50 mg/dL. Higher is better.
Normal TSH range is 0.4 to 4.0 mIU/L. Below 0.4 means overactive thyroid. Above 4.0 means underactive.
Normal creatinine for adults is 0.6 to 1.2 mg/dL. High creatinine suggests kidney stress.
Normal hemoglobin for men is 13.5 to 17.5 g/dL. For women 12 to 15.5 g/dL. Low means anemia.
Normal platelet count is 150000 to 400000 per microliter. Below 150000 is thrombocytopenia.
Normal WBC count is 4500 to 11000 cells per microliter. High WBC means infection or inflammation.
Normal ALT is 7 to 56 units per liter. High ALT means the liver is stressed or damaged.
Vitamin D deficiency is below 20 ng/mL. Insufficiency is 20 to 29 ng/mL. Normal is above 30 ng/mL.
Vitamin B12 normal range is 200 to 900 pg/mL. Below 200 causes nerve damage and anemia.
Normal uric acid for men is 3.4 to 7.0 mg/dL. For women 2.4 to 6.0 mg/dL. High causes gout.
Normal serum sodium is 136 to 145 mEq/L. Abnormal sodium causes brain and nerve problems.
Normal potassium is 3.5 to 5.0 mEq/L. Low potassium causes muscle cramps and heart issues.
eGFR above 90 means normal kidney function. Below 60 for 3 months means chronic kidney disease.
Normal bilirubin is 0.1 to 1.2 mg/dL. High bilirubin causes jaundice and signals liver issues.
Normal albumin is 3.4 to 5.4 g/dL. Low albumin means poor liver function or malnutrition.
Normal ESR for men under 50 is below 15 mm/hr. For women under 50 below 20 mm/hr. High means inflammation.
Normal INR for a healthy person is 0.8 to 1.1. High INR means blood takes longer to clot.
Normal calcium is 8.5 to 10.2 mg/dL. High calcium can mean parathyroid or bone problems.
Normal RBC count for men is 4.7 to 6.1 million per microliter. For women 4.2 to 5.4.
Normal blood pressure is below 120 over 80 mmHg. Above 140 over 90 is hypertension stage 2.
Normal resting heart rate is 60 to 100 beats per minute. Below 60 is bradycardia.
Normal body temperature is 36.1 to 37.2 degrees Celsius. Above 38 is fever.
Triglycerides below 150 mg/dL is normal. Above 200 is high. Above 500 is very high risk.
Normal total cholesterol is below 200 mg/dL. Above 240 is high risk for heart disease.
Iron deficiency anemia shows low hemoglobin, low ferritin, and high TIBC together.
Thyroid peroxidase antibodies above 35 IU/mL suggest autoimmune thyroid disease.
Normal fasting insulin is 2 to 25 mIU/L. High fasting insulin indicates insulin resistance.
Normal AST is 8 to 48 units per liter. High AST indicates liver damage or muscle injury.
Normal Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) is 44 to 147 IU/L. High ALP points to bile duct blockage or bone disease.
Normal GGT is 9 to 48 units per liter. High GGT points to liver disease, often related to alcohol or bile ducts.
Normal total protein is 6.0 to 8.3 g/dL. Low protein may indicate liver or kidney disease.
Globulin levels typically range from 2.0 to 3.5 g/dL. High levels suggest chronic inflammation or infection.
Blood urea nitrogen (BUN) normal range is 7 to 20 mg/dL. High values indicate poor kidney function.
A high BUN-to-creatinine ratio (above 20:1) can indicate dehydration or gastrointestinal bleeding.
Normal Hematocrit is 38.3 to 48.6 percent for men and 35.5 to 44.9 percent for women.
Mean corpuscular volume (MCV) normal range is 80 to 100 fL. High MCV indicates macrocytic anemia, low indicates microcytic.
Mean corpuscular hemoglobin (MCH) normal range is 27 to 33 picograms per cell.
Red cell distribution width (RDW) normal range is 11.8 to 15.6 percent. High RDW means varying red blood cell sizes.
Normal fasting C-peptide is 0.5 to 2.0 ng/mL. Low levels indicate lower insulin production, commonly seen in Type 1 Diabetes.
Free T3 normal range is 2.3 to 4.1 pg/mL. High levels indicate hyperthyroidism.
Free T4 normal range is 0.9 to 1.7 ng/dL. Low levels suggest primary hypothyroidism.
Normal Magnesium is 1.7 to 2.2 mg/dL. Low magnesium can cause muscle spasms and arrhythmias.
Normal serum iron is 60 to 170 mcg/dL. High levels may indicate hemochromatosis.
Normal Ferritin is 24 to 336 ng/mL for men and 11 to 307 ng/mL for women. Low ferritin confirms iron deficiency.
Normal Transferrin saturation is 15 to 50 percent. Below 15 percent suggests low iron availability.
High-sensitivity CRP (hs-CRP) under 1.0 mg/L means low risk of cardiovascular disease. Above 3.0 means high risk.
Creatine kinase (CK) normal range is 22 to 198 U/L. Elevated CK means muscle damage.
Troponin levels are normally undetectable or very low. Elevated troponin strongly indicates an acute heart attack.
Brain natriuretic peptide (BNP) below 100 pg/mL is normal. Above 400 pg/mL strongly suggests heart failure.
Lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) normal range is 140 to 280 U/L. High LDH is a marker for tissue damage or hemolysis.
Homocysteine normal range is less than 15 micromol/L. High levels increase the risk of vascular disease.
Normal serum phosphorus is 2.5 to 4.5 mg/dL. Imbalance often points to chronic kidney disease or parathyroid issues.
Prostate-specific antigen (PSA) is normally below 4.0 ng/mL for men. Higher values may warrant investigation for prostate issues.
Normal aPTT is 25 to 35 seconds. High values mean blood is taking longer to clot.
D-Dimer is normally less than 0.50 mg/L FEU. High levels indicate the presence of significant blood clots.
Normal Chloride is 96 to 106 mEq/L. It works with sodium to maintain fluid balance.
Folate (Vitamin B9) normal range is 2.7 to 17.0 ng/mL. Low folate causes megaloblastic anemia.
